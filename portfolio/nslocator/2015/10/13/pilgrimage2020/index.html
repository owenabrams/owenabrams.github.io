Foot Pilgrimage 2020
What has been sketched above represents the exact trail and events that marked the final journey of Archbishop Janani Luwum. The idea behind planned “Foot Pilgrimage 2020” is to retrace this martyrdom journey, its itinerary and milestones; station by station, as it actually unfolded historically. It is to evoke today the spiritual and historical moments that defined this searing martyrdom.

The purpose of “Foot Pilgrimage 2020” is grateful remembrance and thanksgiving for the extraordinary life and example of Archbishop Janani, for his faith and courage, selflessness and sacrifice, humility and love. Drawing on his example and the Scriptures, the Pilgrimage will be an opportunity to renew and deepen our faith. All Christians, drawn from their various faith traditions (Anglican, Catholic, Orthodox, Pentecostal, Baptist, Born-Again, etc.) have been warmly invited to participate in this journey of faith. Led by the Church of Uganda, this Pilgrimage will be a coming together for all Christians who feel called to undertake this devotion in unity, humility and common fellowship. Most important, the Pilgrimage prompts each of us to ask of ourselves particularly: What should I learn and emulate from the extraordinary life and example of this hero of faith?

This foot Pilgrimage will be a prayerful walk of faith, tracing the final martyrdom journey of St Janani – – this towering hero and compelling role model for all, regardless of faith traditions. This Pilgrimage is a homage, in grateful remembrance of St Janani’s faith, courage and searing martyrdom . During the Pilgrimage, a team of spiritual leaders will be on hand to provide deeper teaching and exposition of Scripture . This will be a time for the renewal of our faith. This Pilgrimage will be an amazing historical and spiritual sojourn.

The foot Pilgrimage will also be the occasion for the launch of a major fundraising campaign for the development of Wii Gweng (building suitable structures and facilities), as a befitting memorial site for St Janani, and for national and international commemoration and pilgrimage.

Participation in the Pilgrimage
All Christians, drawn from their various faith traditions (Anglican, Roman Catholic, Orthodox, Pentecostal, Born-Again, Baptist, etc,), are warmly welcome to participate in this walk of faith. This Pilgrimage is a coming together for all Christians, in unity, humility, and common fellowship.

Route, Distance & Duration
The foot pilgrims will set off from Namirembe Cathedral, proceed to Serena Hotel, then on to Nakasero, before embarking on the long trek along the Kampala­ Bombo-Gulu-Kitgum national highway. From Kitgum town, the pilgrims will walk past Mucwini Chua, proceeding to Madi Opei first, before returning, in the final leg, to the martyr’s graveside at Wii Gweng. The total distance that will be covered in the Pilgrimage, tracing exactly St Janani’s final journey, is 510 kilometers. The plan is to walk an average of 35 kilometers per day. Sundays will be reserved for rest and prayers.

On 26 January, 2020, a service for the blessing of the Pilgrimage and the pilgrims will take place at Namirembe Cathedral. In the early morning of 27 January, the foot pilgrims will be flagged off from Namirembe. Pilgrims from different localities will need to make their way to Kampala, in time for the service of blessing on 26 January, and the start of the journey on 27 January. It is estimated that the pilgrims will arrive at Wii Gweng on 12 February. Altogether, the Pilgrimage will have taken some 15 walking days and 2 days (Sundays) of rest. The foot Pilgrimage will also be the major prelude activity, leading up to the annual commemoration of St Janani Luwum Day 2020, on 16 February.

Registration & Deadline
All intending pilgrims should register, at an early opportunity, through their parishes, by notifying their priests or heads of laity. Pilgrims from outside Uganda may register directly through the Pilgrimage secretariat. The final date for registration is 15 November, 2019. However, in order to facilitate good planning, intending pilgrims are urged to register at their earliest opportunity, preferably well before the set deadline of 15 November.

Some contribution from Pilgrims
In order to help defray the overall costs for the Pilgrimage, each pilgrim is requested to make a modest financial contribution of a minimum of 75,000 shillings (seventy-five thousand Uganda shillings). It is hoped that many pilgrims will be able to contribute more generously than the minimum amount indicated. In addition, each pilgrim is being requested to contribute 5 kilograms of beans and 3 kilograms of sugar.

Sponsor a Pilgrim
There are many Christians who would very much like to join the Pilgrimage, but for reasons beyond their control, may not be able to do so. However, they can still play a very important role by contributing to the development fund for building structures and facilities at Wii Gweng. They can also sponsor a pilgrim or two. Sponsoring a pilgrim means two things: making the financial contribution on behalf of a named pilgrim, and paying the one-way bus fare, from her/his residential locality to Kampala. The sponsorship will support intending pilgrims who are ready to participate, but lack the resources for financial contribution and bus fare.

Role of Parishes
The various parishes and congregations will serve as the focal points for this Pilgrimage. They will play the key role of informing, mobilizing and organizing their congregations. They will serve as the main centers for registration. They can also play a very useful role in organizing sponsorship, where necessary, to enable members of their congregations to participate in the pilgrimage.

Joining the Pilgrimage along the route
In general, it is hoped that most intending pilgrims, who are able to, will plan to begin the Pilgrimage from the beginning – – at Namirembe. However, it is understandable that there will be other Christians who are keen to participate, but can only join in along the route. They are welcome to do so. This will need to be done in organized groups, registering through a parish or diocese.The following locations/towns along the route (on the national highway) have been designated as entry points for pilgrims joining along the way: Migeera, Kigumba, Corner Kamdini, Gulu, Atanga, Acoli-bur, Kitgum. For this group of pilgrims, each is requested to contribute a minimum of 30,000 shillings (thirty-thousand Uganda shillings). Each also is requested to bring 3 kilograms of beans and 2 kilograms of sugar.

Welfare of pilgrims
Provisions and facilities along the route will be quite basic, but adequate. However, participants should be prepared to put up with some measure of hardship. This is in the nature of a pilgrimage. The staple diet will be beans, with posho (maize meal) or rice; there may be occasional variation. There will be two meals a day – – a hearty brunch, on the road, in late morning; and supper, at the end of day, at the location of overnight rest. Overnight rest will typically be organized in near-by primary school classrooms and church sanctuaries. In preparing for this journey, pilgrims also should remember that the journey will coincide with the peak of the dry-and-hot season in Uganda, when temperatures can rise quite high.

Things to bring along
It is advisable to travel very light, with only a few of the most essential items. Each pilgrim is advised to include the following items in her/his safari bag: a plastic cup & a plastic plate; a small jerrycan or container (for water); sleeping gear (including a mat for the floor) ; and a few essential clothes, which are easy-to-wash and fast­ to-dry. A light back-pack bag may be handy. It is especially important to have very comfortable walking shoes; many prefer to walk with open shoes or slippers. Pilgrims also are advised to carry some modest pocket money, for contingencies and little snacks along the way.

Health matters
This Pilgrimage will be demanding. However, if a pilgrim is in general good health and feels motivated to undertake this journey of faith, it is entirely doable. As a precautionary measure, it is necessary for all pilgrims to undertake proper general medical checkup prior to embarking on this arduous trek; sometimes we may have medical conditions which are not apparent to us. There will be provision for First Aid and minor contingencies. For more involved interventions, arrangements will be made for support from medical teams in hospitals along the route. It also is hoped that some trained medical professionals might be among the foot pilgrims.

Planning & Secretariat for the Pilgrimage
There will be a central planning committee, based in Kampala. Other planning teams will be constituted to help coordinate activities at regional and local levels.
The Provincial Secretary of the Church of Uganda has kindly offered his office, in Namirembe, to serve as the secretariat for the Pilgrimage.

About SJLM

SJLM's overall objective is to plan and put in place memorial arrangements, to honor, remember and celebrate the Life, Testimony, Martyrdom and Example of Won-wa, St Janani Luwum, as a preeminent Christian leader and 20th Century martyr. He is indeed a most compelling role model for the world, regardless of faith background.

MEMORIAL CENTER

Part of the work being done by SJLM is the creation, establishment, protection and preservation of a Center of Memory about Saint Janani Jakaliya Luwum, which contains an archive of the life and times, works and writings of the late Archbishop of the Metropolitan Province of Uganda, Rwanda, Burundi, and Boga (in Zaire) from 1974 until his death in 1977.

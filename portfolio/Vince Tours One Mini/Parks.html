MurchinsonFALLS National Park

"The World's Most Powerful Waterfall" Highlights: Launch Trip to Murchinson Falls, game viewing in the Buligi savannah, Chimpanzee tracking in Budongo forest

Uganda's largest park is named after the Nile's dramatic plunge through a narrow cleft in the rift valley scarp. The river is home to hundreds of Hippos, Crocodiles and waterbirds while big game lives in the grassland north of the river and primates in forests to south.

Conservation Importance

Giraffes, Buffaloes, Elephants, Uganda Kobs, Hyenas, Oribis,Hartebeests, Lions, and leopards.

The park also protects a 20-30km stretch of River Nile as a breeding area for fish, hippos, Nile crocodiles and water birds. Ecosystem types range from savannah grassland, woodland, tropical rainforests, rivers and swamps. In this wide range of habitat, 479 species of birds have been recorded, 87 species of mammals and 14 species of reptiles, fish and butterflies.

Tourist Attraction

The Murchinson Falls

The falls are a major attraction in this park and can be accessed by boat on River Nile from Paraa to the bottom of the falls, or by road to the top of the falls. Along the 7km river stretch, are plenty of water birds, Hippos, Crocodiles, Bush-bucks, Water-bucks, Lions, Leopards, Jackals, Warthogs, Buffaloes, Hartebeests, Kobs, Oribis, Giraffes, and Elephants. All these can be seen while on the game reserve.

QueenELIZABETH National Park

"A Medley of Wonders" Highlights: Rift valley scenery, Kazinga Channel, Kazinga game drive, Tree climbing Lions, Katwe explosion craters

Queen Elizabeth National Park enjoys a fabulous rift valley location at the foot of Mt. Rwenzori between Lake Georfe and Lake Edward. Savannah, Acacia woodland, forest, wetland, and open water support a wide range of animals and a remarkable 604 bird species.

Conservation Importance

World Bio-sphere reserve (UNESCO 1979), RAMSAR wetland site, and classiified as Important Bird Area (IBA) by Birdlife International.

Most area is covered by water, thickets rain forests, swamps and savanna grasslands. This is a habitat for atleast 98 mammals, 606 bird species and various species of fish, insects & reptiles.

Tourist Attraction

Tree Climbing Lions in Ishasha sector

This park is famous for Tree Climbing Lions in Ishasha sector, Chimpazee tracking in Kyambura, nature walks in Maramagambo forest and boat trips on the Kazinga Channel to the shores of Lake Edward at Mweya peninsula. While on the boat, you can easily see Elephants, Buffaloes, Hippos, Nile Crocodiles, and alot of water birds (over 600 other species) on the shores. The park also hosts Kobs, Lions, Leopards and Hyenas. There are also game drives to Kasenyi matting grounds.

KidepoVALLEY National Park

"True African Wilderness" Highlights: Game viewing, wilderness Scenery

Uganda's finest wilderness lies in the farthest corner of wild Karamoja. Elephants, Buffaloes, Lions, Giraffes, Zebras, Elands, Hartebeests, Hyenas, and Cheetah (etc) roam rolling grasslands that extend in all directions towards distant mountain ranges.

Conservation Importance

Rare endemic regional and globally threatened species of wildlife

These include; the Ostrich, Eland, Roan Antelope, Greater and Lesser Kudu, Dikdik, African wild dog, Leopard, Bat-eared fox, Stripped Hyena, Aardwolf, Caracal and Cheetah. The Beisa Oryx, Roan Antelope and the Bright Gazelle have been greatly reduced in number through poaching

Tourist Attraction

Narus Valley and the seasonal Kidepo River lined with borassus palms and acacia trees

The Narus Valley is a major source of water and pasture for grazers and a hunting ground for carnivores. Here, You can see Elephants, Zebras, Giraffes, Eland, Buffaloes, Hyenas and Lions. Special birds in the park include the Ostrich, Kori Bustard, Karamoja Apalis, Verreaux's Eagle, Pygmy Falcon & the Egyptian Vulture. The seasonal Kidepo river is lined with a riverine "forest" of Borassus Palms and Acacia trees along a sand bed river. This is a prime spot for Dwarf Crocodiles. The park also boosts of atleast 80 species of mammals recorded, 28 of which are not known to appear in any other national park in Uganda as well as 486 bird species including the famous Ostrich.

RwenzoriMOUNTAINS National Park

"The Mystical Challenge" Highlights: Mountain trekking in surreal montane setting, Equatorial Snow Peaks, Afromontane vegetation

The 5100m Mount Rwenzori - or "Mountains of the Moon" is the snowy source of the Nile reffered to by early geographers. The trails to the snow peaks ascend glacier-carved valleys filled with colourful mosses and giant foorms of lobelias, heathers and groundsels.

ConservationImportance

Tropical Rainforest, Alpine Meadows and Snow

The range supports its own species and varieties of giant Lobelia. The Albertine Rift-Valley hosts six endemic small mammals which include the Rwenzori Otter Shrew and Rwenzori Red Duiker. The Mountain supports 19 endemic bird species including the Rwenzori Turaco, and the Blue-headed Sunbird. Endemic reptiles include the Great Lakes Bush Viper and the Strange Horned Chameleon. 212 bird species and 77 mammal species have been recorded in the Rwenzoris.

Tourist Attraction

Mountain Climbing (Up to 7 days), Spectacular Sceneries and unique plant life

Unlike other national parks in Uganda that offer excellent game viewing opportunities, Rwenzori is better known for its spectacular Sceneries and Unique plant life. Mountain climbing is a major activity with trekking along the Rwenzori Central Trail circuit taking up to 7 days. Most notably are the permanent Snow Capped Peaks - Margherita (Speke & Baker Peaks) the highest and Stanley Peak the largest, among 3 other peaks.

BwindiIMPENETRABLE National Park

"The Ultimate Gorilla Experience" Highlights: Mountain Gorilla tracking and Bird watching

This ancient forest in the mountaneous south-west of Uganda contains an impressive biodiversity with 350 birds species, 310 butterfly species, 200 tree species, 51 reptile species, 88 moths species and 120 mammals, mostly notably the rare and unforgetable Mountain Gorilla.

Conservation Importance

Habitat for endangered species - Mountain Gorilla and Chimpanzees as well as 8 distinct bird species that are not found anywhere else in East Africa

With over 346 bird species to choose from, 7 of these species are listed in the IUCN Red Data Book and they include the Dwarf Honeyguide (NT), African Green Broadbill (rare), Kivu Ground Thrush (NT), Forest Ground Thrush (rare), Grauer's Rush Warbler (Vulnerable), Chapin's Flycatcher (rare) and Shelley's Crinson-wing (NT).

Tourist Attraction

Gorilla Tracking

Gorilla tracking is the major tourist activity in this park. ( groups of moutain Gorilla families have been habituated and only 8 tourists booked per day. Forest trails lead to various attractions including rivers, waterfalls, swamps, special vegetation type and to high level of wildlife concentration like monkeys and chimpanzees. Specie diversity is great too, with atleast 120 species of mammals, 346 bird species, 202 butterfly species, 163 tree species, 100 fern species, 27 frog species, chameleons, geckos and many endangered species.

MgahingaGORILLA National Park

"Where Gold Meets Silver" Highlights: Mountain Gorilla and Golden Monkey tracking, Bird watching and Volcanic Scenery

The Virunga Volcanoes, home to half of the world's Mountain Gorillas, are shared between Uganda, Rwanda and Congo. The Utiny (38Km) Mgahinga National Park protects the Ugandan portion on the northern slopes of Mt. Muhavura, Mgahinga and Sabinyo

Conservation Importance

Habitat for Mountain Gorillas and Golden Monkeys

Because of the difficult terrain and the fact that most mammals that live here are nocturnal, they are rarely seen. These include forest buffaloes, elephants, giant forest hogs, bush pigs, duikers, leopards, civetts, golden cats, jackals and porcupines. At least 127 bird species have been recorded.

Tourist Attraction

Gorilla Tracking and Golden Monkey Tracking

There is only one Gorilla family established here and a troop of Golden Monkeys. however, new on the list is the long walk Batwa Heritage Trail and Cave exploration of the ancient Garama Cave. Bird watching is also available and the three mountain ranges provide a good challenge to mountain climbers.

SemlikiNational Park

"The True Birders' Haven" Highlights: Forest walks, Sempaya Hot Springs, Bird watching

On the Rift Valley floor beyond the Rwenzori mountains, the Semliki forest forms the easternmost edge of the Congo jungle. The park is an exciting destination for birders drawn by forest species that include many overspills from Central Africa.

Conservation Importance

Unique fauna and flora stretching from the Congo Ituri forest and West African Butterfly

The park borders the Semuliki and Lamia rivers, which form the border between Uganda and the D.R.Congo. The flora and fauna show strong affinities with the Congo basin forest with many species reaching the eastern limit of their ranges in the Semuliki forest. Suggesting it is the vast Ituri forest that has stretched from Congo into Semuliki in Uganda, coming along with its unique fauna and flora. No wonder 31 species of bird, one specie of primate, and one specie of butterfly are west African and only recorded in Semuliki on the East African part of their ranges.

Tourist Attraction

Unique birdlife and Hot Springs

Birds include the Forest Ground Thrush, Sassi's Olive Greenbul and White-crested Hornbill. There also 53 mammal species such as the Beecroft's flying Squirrel, Water chevrotain, Zenker's flying mice, and nocturnal animals of West Africa. The unique birdlife here is the core reason for birdwatcher's to visit, however, the two hot springs about a kilometer apart are easy stop-overs for tourists. Other attractions include the Chimpanzees, Red Colobus Monkey, De Brazza's Monkey and Dent's Mona Monkey. Here, you can also see Forest Buffaloes, Elephants, Pygmy Hippos, and Leopards.

LakeMBURO National Park

"Whispers of the Wild" Highlights: Game Drives, Horseback Safaris

Wetland and Acacia grassland around Lake Mburo sustain a number of herbivores (Impala, Zebra, Eland and Topi) that are rare or absent in other Ugandan Parks. Its diverse habitats of open savannas, dry savannas, lakes, swamps, rocky outcrops, forests, woodlands, thickets and dry hill sides provide suitable ecosystems to a wide range of fauna and flora.

Conservation Importance

Diverse Habitats

The impala, slender mongoose and bush rat are found only in this park, and not anywhere else in Uganda. A small population of Klipspringer still remains in a few isolated outcrops. African hunting dogs, black rhino, giant forest hogs, roan antelopes and elephants have been extinct from the area. There also lots of bird species in this area.

Tourist Attraction

Diverse habitats

The six lakes in this park are all interconnected by swamps. These wetlands are home to a variety of waterbirds including the rare and endangered African Finfoot and the Shoebill, and mammals including the shy and elusive antelope; the Sitatunga. On a boat trip, you can come closer to such wildlife including hippos, Nile crocodiles and water birds. On a game drive, large mammals like Impalas, Zebras, Buffaloes, Eland, Bush-Bucks, Water-Bucks, Monkeys, Oribi, Duikers, Klipspringer, Bush-Pigs, Leopards and rarely Lions can be seen. Small mammals include rock and tree Hyraxes, Bush Babies and Dwarf Mongoose.

MtELGON National Park

"The World's Largest Mountain Caldera" Highlights: Mountain trekking, Afromontane Scenery, Waterfalls

This massive extinct volcano was once Africa's highest mountain - far exceeding Kilimanjaro's 5891m. though reduced to 4321m, it still rises over 3000m above the plains of eastern Uganda. Trails ascend through forest, bamboo and moorland to a giant caldera, 8km wide, on the summit.

Conservation Importance

Unique fauna and flora, waterfalls, gorges, mesas, cliffs, caves, vast calderas, hot springs and mountain peaks

Elgon's slopes support a rich variety of vegetation that ranges from montane forest to high open moorland studed with giant lobelia and groundsel plants. 42 species of mammals ranging from elephants, leopards, buffaloes, bushpigs, monkeys to a variety of small antelope. 312 bird species including the Black-collared Apalis, GreenTinkerbird, Jackson's Francolin, Vereaux's Eagle, and the endangered Lammergeier can also be found here.

Tourist Attraction

Mountain hiking, primate and bird watching, cave explorations, and camping photography

The major activities in this park are Mountain hiking, primate and bird watching, cave explorations, and camping photography. The most popular areas around Mt Ellgon are the vast caves where frequent night visitors such as elephants, bush-pigs and buffaloes come to lick the natural salt found on the cave walls. There are ancient cave paintings near the trail head at Budadiri, the hot springs - 48 degrees centigrade in the former volcano's crater, and the Sipi falls at the foot of Mt Elgon. There are no roads within the park, and all activities are done on foot. No game drives!

KibaleNational Park

"The Primate Capital of the World" Highlights: Chimpanzee Tracking, Birdwatching and Crater lakes

East of the Rwenzori, the landscape is pocked with beautiful crater lakes and carpeted with tea plantations and the tropical forests of Kibale National Park. Kibale is home to 13 types of primates, including Chimpanzees, Red Colobus Monkey, and 355 bird species.

Conservation Importance

Primate capital of the world

The rare L'Hoest's Monkey live in this forest too and the park's population of elephants travels between Kibale and Queen Elizabeth National Park. Other large mammals within the park include Leopards, Bushpigs, three species of Duiker and two species of Otter. Birdlife here include two species of Pittas (African and Green-breasted). Atleast 71 species of mammals have been recorded, 375 species of birds, 250 species of butterflies, 350 species of trees and shrubs, 27 different species of frogs and toads, and 14 species of snake, most notably the largest African rock python.

Tourist Attraction

Chimpanzees

A single family of Chimpanzee has been habituated for tourism. Chimp tracking/Primate walk and bird watching are major tourist activities here. With well developed nature trails, tourists spend time exploring several kilometers of forest.

Toro-SemlikiWildlife Reserve

Highlights: Wilderness Rift Valley setting, Shoebill viewing in Lake Albert Marshes

One os Uganda's oldest reserves (est. 1962), Toro-Semliki is located on the rift valley floor besides Lake Albert where the Rwenzori mountains and Kijura escarpment create memorable backdrops.

Tourist Attraction

Memorable backdrops

Memorable backdrops created by the Kijura escarpment and the great rift valley. Lake Albert also boosts of its iconic rare Shoebill.

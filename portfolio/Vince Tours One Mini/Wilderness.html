In the Wilderness (9 Days)

Birds, Gorillas, Water Falls, Big Five and more Wild Game

This safari will take you through a journey of discovering a quarter of the world’s bird’s population and also the Mighty Mountain Gorilla (Uganda has recorded a steady increase of the gorillas thus housing half of the world’s population). To the west, visit Murchison falls National Park famous for Murchison Falls, and a variety of wild game. Queen Elizabeth National Park that has high concentration of migratory birds, chimp tracking and the big five as well then transfer Bwindi Impenetrable National Park for the gorillas thereafter head to Lake Mburo National park before carrying memories back home.


Day 1: Arrival and check in at the Hotel
Depending on time of arrival at Entebbe Airport, you’ll be welcomed by Grey Horizons’ professionally trained driver guide and transferred to Hotel in Kampala. Optional visit to Uganda wildlife Educational Centre (UWEC) before checking in at the hotel, this shows samples of all the animals in our parks apart from the Gorillas Meal Plan: Half board.

Day 2: Kampala to Murchison Falls NP via Ziwa (9-10hours drive)
Early in the morning after a heavy breakfast, you will then drive to the biggest national park Mighty Murchison falls National Park. Pass through plantations and lots of green, Lunch in Masindi Arriving in the afternoon at ziwa rhino sanctuary. Then proceed to Murchison retiring at the lodge right in time for dinner and relaxation. Meal Plan: Full Board.

Day 3: Murchison Falls NP game drive and boat cruise (Day’s activity)
Our morning safari of Murchison National park starts around 6:30am, with an early morning breakfast or packed breakfast. Drive to the northern bank for a thrilling game drive. Expect to see the some of the big 5 (Elephant, Buffalo, Lion, Leopard) and other wildlife animals not forgetting the Giraffe; you will also have a panoramic view of Lake Albert. Later, return for lunch. After lunch in the afternoon, take a boat cruise to the bottom of the mighty falls; you will see Hippos, crocodiles, elephants and other animals, as well as a variety of bird species. Meal Plan: Full Board.

Day 4: Murchison Falls NP to Fort Portal (8-9 hours)
Early morning, start with a good breakfast to give you the energy needed to head further south on our day’s drive. We’ll head to fort portal (this is an 8-9 hours drive). Stopovers in Hoima then proceed for our dinner and overnight. Meal Plan: Full Board.

Day 5: Queen Elizabeth National Park and Evening Game Drive
After breakfast, we’ll head to Queen Elizabeth crossing the Equator into the park having a game drive upon arrival with a clear view of the western arm of the Great Rift Valley & its associated features such as katwe salt lakes, craters, Lake George and Lake Edward, plus the various wildlife typical to this habitat like the savannah elephant, buffalo, lion, and impala. Lots of different bird species can also be seen here as this is home to three quarters of Africa's bird population. Later in the evening we’ll enjoy a boat cruise then camp. Meal Plan: Full Board .

Day 6: Queen Elizabeth to Bwindi via Ishasha (6-8 hours)
Early morning, start with a good breakfast head further south on our day’s drive. We’ll head to Ishasha (this is a 2 hours drive) where we’ll try our luck upon the search of Ishasha tree climbing lions (2-3hrs game drive).With our packed lunch, we’ll stop for a picnic at the park quarters or some other point on the route where we’re all ready and ready to stop. Thereafter we’ll head to the lodge (2 hours drive) arriving in the evening, in time for dinner and overnight. Meal Plan: Full Board.

Day 7: Gorilla Tracking
Early morning, have breakfast to give you the energy needed for tracking gorillas, a reasonable degree of fitness is required for the Experience. With your packed lunch, you will report to the ranger station for registration and a talk by one of the rangers by 8:00am. You will trek through thick creepers and undergrowth with your guides to find the Gorillas, which trek can take from half an hour to 9 hours. The steeply forested landscape is incredibly dense but crisscrossed by numerous animal trails allowing access to tourists. It’s amazing to sit, watch and photograph the gorillas in the forest besides, listening to them marvel and grumble to each other. It is an extraordinary feeling being with these beautiful yet endangered Creatures. Meal Plan: Full Board.

Day 8: Bwindi to Lake Mburo NP
Today we start with an early breakfast, then check out and begin our transfer towards Mbarara (6 hours drive).Our target destination is Lake Mburo National Park, about 45km from Mbarara. Meal Plan: Full Board.

Day 9: Lake Mburo NP (Mihingo lodge) to Entebbe
After our morning breakfast we head out for a game drive in Lake Mburo national park with its underlain ancient Precambrian metamorphic rocks which date back more than 500 million years. It is home to 350 bird species as well as zebra, impala, eland, buffalo, oribi, Defassa waterbuck, hippos, hyena, topi and reedbuck. Lunch at the equator if time allows as we’ll dictated by departure time at the airport.
End of tour.
